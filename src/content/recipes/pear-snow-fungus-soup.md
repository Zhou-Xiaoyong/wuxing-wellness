In five-element practice, autumn is ruled by Metal and associated with "dryness" — dry cough, dry skin, dry stools. This moist, white-toned soup is the classic counter.

This is food, not medicine.

## Ingredients

- 1 dried snow fungus (Tremella), soaked and torn
- 2 pears, cored and cubed
- 6 dried red dates, pitted
- 5 cups water
- A few goji berries

## Method

1. Simmer snow fungus in water 40 minutes until gelatinous.
2. Add pears and dates; simmer 20 more minutes.
3. Stir in goji berries off heat.
4. Serve warm. Keeps in the fridge 2–3 days.

## When to reach for it

Traditional practice pairs this with Metal-time signals: a tickly throat, dry skin, or a low, held-in sadness in autumn. A moist, soothing bowl — not a treatment.
