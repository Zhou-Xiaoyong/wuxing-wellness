If you've ever felt puffy, heavy-limbed, and vaguely swollen after a run of humid days or too much takeout, Chinese wellness has a name for it — *damp* — and a drink for it.

This is that drink. Pearl barley (薏米, *yì yǐ rén*) and adzuki beans (红豆, *hóng dòu*) are simmered into a clean, almost neutral water that you sip through the day instead of reaching for another soda. No sugar, no milk, no ceremony. It is the pantry equivalent of opening a window.

## Ingredients

- 1/2 cup pearl barley (coix seed / job's tears)
- 1/3 cup adzuki beans
- 1.2 L water
- A small piece of dried tangerine peel (optional, for digestion)

## Method

1. Soak the barley and adzuki beans in water for at least 2 hours, or overnight.
2. Rinse and add to a pot with the 1.2 L water.
3. Bring to a boil, then lower to a gentle simmer for 40 minutes — the beans should be soft and the water faintly cloudy.
4. Strain. Drink the liquid warm or at room temperature through the day.
5. You can eat the soft beans too, or leave them out — the water is the point.

## Why it works

Barley and adzuki are the workhorses of the "drain dampness" tradition in Chinese diet therapy — both appear in classic texts as foods that move water and lighten the body without harsh purging. In five-element terms this is Earth-phase maintenance: the spleen runs on warmth and dryness, and a damp spell makes it sluggish. This water tips the balance back.

## When to reach for it

- Puffy eyes, soft swelling in the hands or ankles.
- That "lead blanket" fatigue on muggy days.
- After a stretch of heavy, greasy, or late meals.
- As a daily替换 for sugary drinks during humid seasons.

Important: traditionally coix/barley is avoided in pregnancy. This is a dietary ritual, not a treatment — if you have kidney issues, are pregnant, or take medication, check with your practitioner first.
