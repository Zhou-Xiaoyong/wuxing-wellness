Turmeric sits at the crossroads of Indian and Chinese food traditions. In five-element terms it's a warm, "pungent" spice near Earth and Fire — used to dispel cold and support the center.

This is food, not medicine. Turmeric can interact with blood thinners — check with a clinician if that applies.

## Ingredients

- 1 cup milk of choice (dairy, oat or coconut)
- 1/2 tsp ground turmeric
- Tiny pinch ground black pepper
- 1/4 tsp grated ginger
- Optional: dash of cinnamon, half tsp honey

## Method

1. Warm the milk (don't boil) in a small pot.
2. Whisk in turmeric, pepper and ginger until smooth.
3. Off heat, add cinnamon/honey if using.
4. Sip warm, slowly, away from screens.

## When to reach for it

Traditional practice pairs this with cold, stiff or low-mood days. A cozy ritual — not a treatment, and not a substitute for prescribed care.
