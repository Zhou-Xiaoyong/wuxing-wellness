In five-element practice, black foods nourish the Water element and the kidneys — the body's store of deep energy. This soup leans into that idea for cold months.

This is food, not medicine.

## Ingredients

- 1 cup black beans, soaked overnight
- 1/4 cup walnuts
- 3 slices ginger
- 5 cups water
- Pinch of sea salt

## Method

1. Simmer black beans and ginger 45 minutes until tender.
2. Add walnuts in the last 10 minutes.
3. Mash a few beans against the pot for body; salt to taste.
4. Serve hot, especially on cold evenings.

## When to reach for it

Traditional practice pairs this with Water-time signals: cold hands and feet, low-back ache, or fatigue that sleep doesn't fix in winter. A warming meal — not a treatment.
