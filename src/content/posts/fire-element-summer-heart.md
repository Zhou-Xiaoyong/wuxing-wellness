If Wood is the planner, **Fire** is the spark. In five-element theory, Fire is the energy of summer — brightness, warmth and expression. It is linked with the heart and the small intestine, and with the emotion of joy (and, at the extreme, agitation).

This is a cultural wellness perspective, not medical advice. Chest pain, palpitations or breathlessness should always be checked by a clinician.

## The Fire type at a glance

Fire people tend to be:

- **Expressive and warm** — they light up a room and love connection.
- **Quick-minded** — fast talkers, fast thinkers, charmers.
- **Sensitive to overstimulation** — too much heat, caffeine or late nights leaves them wired.

When Fire is balanced, these traits become enthusiasm and genuine warmth. When it runs "hot," the same energy shows up as restlessness, poor sleep, a racing mind and a flushed, agitated feeling.

## Quick self-check: is your Fire running hot?

| Sign | You? |
| --- | --- |
| Trouble falling or staying asleep | ☐ |
| Racing thoughts at night | ☐ |
| Easily overheated or flushed | ☐ |
| Mouth ulcers in summer | ☐ |
| Restless, can't sit still | ☐ |
| Craves connection but burns out fast | ☐ |

A couple of ticks in a heat wave is normal. Many of them together is a nudge to cool down.

## Summer habits for Fire balance

1. **Cool, not iced.** Room-temperature or lightly cooled foods suit the heart better than icy drinks in this view.
2. **Protect the evenings.** Screens and late stimulation feed Fire's restlessness; a wind-down routine helps.
3. **Bitter greens.** Traditional Fire foods include bitter melon, romaine and dandelion — "bitter clears heat."
4. **Pace social energy.** Fire types love people; build in recovery so warmth doesn't flip to burnout.

## Recipe: lotus-seed & mung-bean cooling tea

A gentle, caffeine-free summer drink from the Chinese kitchen — bitter and sweet, meant to take the "edge" off heat.

**Ingredients**
- 2 tbsp dried mung beans (green)
- 1 tbsp dried lotus seeds
- 4 cups water
- 2–3 dried lily bulbs (optional)
- A few goji berries to finish

**Method**
1. Rinse mung beans and lotus seeds; simmer in water 25–30 minutes until soft.
2. Add lily bulbs in the last 10 minutes if using.
3. Strain into a pitcher; cool to room temperature.
4. Top each glass with a few goji berries.

This is a refreshing food ritual, not a treatment. It simply leans into "cooling" flavors traditional for summer.

## The heart in five-element thinking

Fire's organ is the heart, often called the "emperor" of the body in classical texts — the seat of spirit and calm. Supporting Fire isn't about stimulation; it's about *not overheating* the system that's meant to stay steady.
