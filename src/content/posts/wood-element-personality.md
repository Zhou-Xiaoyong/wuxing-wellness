If the five elements were people, **Wood** would be the one with a plan, a deadline and a restless need to move. In traditional Chinese wellness, Wood is the energy of spring — growth, vision and upward push. In the body it is linked with the liver and gallbladder, the organs associated with the smooth flow of energy and emotion.

This article is about the *Wood personality type* as described in five-element theory — a framework that has been part of Chinese culture for more than two thousand years. It is a lens for self-understanding, not a diagnosis, and nothing here is medical advice.

## The Wood type at a glance

Wood people tend to be:

- **Ambitious and decisive** — they like to be first, best and original.
- **Natural planners** — comfortable directing and pushing through pressure.
- **Impatient when blocked** — stalled plans quickly turn to frustration.

When Wood energy flows well, these traits become drive and initiative. When it stagnates, the same energy shows up as irritability, tight shoulders, headaches and a short fuse.

## Why "anger" belongs to Wood

Each element has an emotion tied to it. Wood's is **anger** — not as a character flaw, but as a signal. A small amount of anger is the spark that drives change; chronic irritation usually means energy isn't moving the way the liver wants it to.

> Think of Wood like a plant: healthy growth needs space. Crowd it, and it competes aggressively. The fix is rarely to "calm down" — it's to move.

## Simple habits for Wood balance

1. **Move daily.** Walking, Qi Gong or stretching keeps liver energy smooth in the traditional view.
2. **Eat your greens.** Leafy vegetables and sour foods (lemon, sprouts) are the traditional Wood supporters.
3. **Protect sleep.** The liver is said to do its deepest work at night; a regular schedule matters more than you'd think.
4. **Name the block.** Much Wood tension is unspoken frustration. Writing it down externalizes it.
