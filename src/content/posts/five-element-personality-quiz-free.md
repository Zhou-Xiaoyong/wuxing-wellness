The free five-element personality quiz is the fastest way to see your profile laid out in numbers. But a score is only useful if you know what it's measuring — and what it isn't. This short guide explains how the quiz works and, just as importantly, how to read it without turning a helpful reflection into a rigid label.

As with everything on this site, the quiz is a cultural reflection tool, not a psychological or medical assessment. If you're struggling with your mental health, please talk to a licensed professional.

## How the quiz works

The quiz asks **20 questions**, split evenly so that **4 questions map to each element** — Wood, Fire, Earth, Metal, and Water. For every statement you rate how true it feels on a scale from 1 (not me) to 5 (very me).

Your answers produce five scores, one per element. The element with the highest total is your **dominant** type; the runner-up is your **secondary**. Because the questions are grouped by element, a high Wood score means your "planner who hates being blocked" answers lined up — not that you're literally a tree.

## What "dominant" and "secondary" mean

Most people aren't one pure type. A typical result looks like **Wood-dominant with a Fire secondary**: ambitious and initiative-driven, but also social and expressive. That combination explains more than either label alone.

A few things to keep in mind:

- **A small gap is normal.** If Wood is 18 and Fire is 17, you're genuinely both — don't force a single answer.
- **Low scores aren't weaknesses.** A low Metal score doesn't mean you're messy; it means that energy isn't your go-to. You can still cultivate it.
- **Scores shift with the season.** People often test more "Wood" in spring and more "Water" in winter. Retake it across the year if you're curious.

## Reading your result without labels

The point of the quiz isn't to sort yourself into a box. It's to notice patterns you can actually use:

- If Wood leads, your stress relief is probably *movement*, not analysis.
- If Fire leads, you likely need connection and rest in equal measure.
- If Earth leads, routine and being needed matter more than you admit.
- If Metal leads, boundaries and "good enough" will protect your energy.
- If Water leads, unstructured downtime isn't laziness — it's how you refill.
