"I'm tired but wired" is one of the most common modern complaints — and in five-element theory it has a neat explanation. When the deep, storing energy of **Water** (linked with the kidneys) runs low, the body can't fully "close down" at night. The result is exactly that: exhausted but unable to switch off.

This is a cultural self-care perspective, not medical advice. Chronic insomnia should be reviewed by a clinician.

## The Water–sleep link

Water is the element of storage and stillness; winter is its season. In this framework the kidneys hold the body's reserve energy, and restful sleep depends on that reserve being topped up. Overwork, late nights and constant stimulation drain it — and the nervous system stays alert when it should be settling.

## Quick self-check: is "Water depletion" part of your sleep story?

| Sign | You? |
| --- | --- |
| Wired-tired at bedtime | ☐ |
| Wake 2–4 a.m. and can't return | ☐ |
| Low back ache, cold feet | ☐ |
| Dread or vague anxiety at night | ☐ |
| Rely on screens to "wind down" | ☐ |
| Better sleep in winter/by the sea | ☐ |

A few ticks is a useful pattern to notice, not a diagnosis.

## A Water-style wind-down routine

1. **Lights low by 9.** Dim the environment an hour before bed; bright light feeds Fire's restlessness.
2. **Screens off.** The single biggest lever — replace with reading or stretching.
3. **Warm the feet.** Socks or a foot bath; cold feet keep the system alert.
4. **Slow breathing.** Six belly breaths to signal "store, don't spend."
5. **Same wake time.** A fixed rise time anchors the whole cycle more than a fixed bedtime.

## Recipe: black-sesame & walnut night drink

Black sesame is a traditional Water/kidney food in Chinese food therapy — warm, dark and "nourishing."

**Ingredients**
- 2 tbsp black sesame paste (or ground toasted seeds)
- 1 tbsp walnut pieces
- 1 cup warm milk of choice
- Optional: few drops honey

**Method**
1. Whisk sesame paste into warm milk until smooth.
2. Stir in walnut pieces.
3. Sip warm, slowly, as the last thing before bed.

A soothing food ritual, not a treatment.

## The bigger picture

In five-element thinking, sleep isn't just "rest" — it's the daily act of storing energy for tomorrow. A Water-supportive night routine is less about forcing sleep and more about letting the system bank what it needs.
