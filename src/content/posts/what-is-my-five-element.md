If you've landed here wondering "what is my five element," you're in good company. The five elements — Wood (木), Fire (火), Earth (土), Metal (金), and Water (水) — are the backbone of a way of reading people that has been part of Chinese culture for more than two thousand years. It started as a way to understand nature and the body, laid out in the *Yellow Emperor's Inner Canon* (《黄帝内经》), and over centuries it grew into a gentle typology: a lens for noticing your rhythms, your stress patterns, and what restores you.

This is not a diagnosis and not medical advice. Think of it like learning your sleep chronotype or attachment style — a useful story, not a cage. Most people are a *blend*, with one element loudest and a second close behind.

## The five elements at a glance

Each element pairs an energy with a season, a temperament, and a part of the body:

- **Wood** — the energy of spring: growth, vision, and forward push. Wood people plan, initiate, and get restless when blocked. In the body it's linked with the liver.
- **Fire** — the energy of summer: warmth, expression, and connection. Fire people light up a room and burn out fast when overextended. It's linked with the heart.
- **Earth** — the energy of late summer: nourishment, routine, and caretaking. Earth people hold things together and worry when things feel unstable. It's linked with the spleen and digestion.
- **Metal** — the energy of autumn: refinement, boundaries, and letting go. Metal people value quality and order, and turn inward when overwhelmed. It's linked with the lungs.
- **Water** — the energy of winter: rest, depth, and endurance. Water people are private, resourceful, and slow to trust, but steady once committed. It's linked with the kidneys.

## Three fast ways to guess your type

You don't need any tools to start.

1. **Notice your birth season — or the season you feel most alive in.** People often feel most "themselves" in their element's season.
2. **Watch your default stress reaction.** Do you get *angry and driven* (Wood), *wired and scattered* (Fire), *worried and clingy* (Earth), *withdrawn and crisp* (Metal), or *frozen and tired* (Water)?
3. **Track what restores you.** A long walk, a good laugh, a home-cooked meal, quiet solitude, a deep sleep — your go-to reset is a strong clue.
